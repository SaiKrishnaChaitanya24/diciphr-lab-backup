Nima Broomand Lomer
Postdoctoral Researcher
Nima is a dedicated postdoctoral research scholar with a deep passion for radiomics, artificial intelligence, and their innovative applications in oncology and neuroimaging. He earned his medical degree from Guilan University of Medical Sciences in December 2020 and began his research career focusing on Multiple Sclerosis and brain tumors. In September 2024, he joined the University of Pennsylvania to further his academic and professional pursuits. Currently, he is a postdoc at the DiCIPHR Lab within the Department of Radiology. His current work focuses on brain tumors and traumatic brain injuries, utilizing radiomics and advanced tractography techniques to optimize treatment planning. With extensive expertise in machine learning, data analysis, and tractography, Nima is committed to advancing early diagnosis and intervention through cutting-edge imaging technologies. His goal is to become a radiologist, contributing to improved healthcare outcomes through innovative research and clinical excellence.
